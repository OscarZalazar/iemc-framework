# Use Cases

This document describes public research use cases for IEMC Framework. All examples are synthetic.

## Understanding Irony

Irony often requires contradiction between literal content and contextual meaning.

Example:

```text
"Genial, otra reunion mas."
```

A text-only system may classify this as positive because of "genial". A multimodal cultural analysis may consider tone, timing, workplace context, facial expression, and regional conversational style.

Research value:

- Improves pragmatic interpretation.
- Reduces false positive sentiment labels.
- Helps assistants respond with appropriate empathy.

## Understanding Humor

Humor can depend on setup, surprise, exaggeration, rhythm, and shared references.

Example:

```text
A creator calmly describes a minor inconvenience as if it were a national emergency.
```

The humor may not be in the words alone. It may come from visual seriousness, vocal restraint, editing rhythm, and cultural familiarity with dramatic exaggeration.

Research value:

- Better video understanding.
- Improved content categorization.
- More accurate tone-aware summarization.

## Viral Videos

Viral content often works because several layers align:

- Immediate emotional hook
- Recognizable cultural reference
- Strong visual pattern
- Short narrative arc
- Shareable identity signal

IEMC can help describe why a video resonates without reducing it to raw engagement metrics.

Research value:

- Media intelligence
- Creative analysis
- Communication studies
- Ethical AdTech research

## Latin American Cultural Context

Latin American communication often includes rich regional variation in humor, irony, warmth, exaggeration, indirect critique, and code-switching.

Example:

```text
"Tranqui, no pasa nada."
```

Depending on tone and context, this can mean genuine reassurance, contained frustration, social politeness, or ironic understatement.

Research value:

- Better support for regional Spanish.
- More culturally aware AI assistants.
- Reduced bias from English-centric emotion datasets.

## Emotional Analysis

IEMC supports emotional interpretation as a hypothesis-building process.

Example:

```text
Scene: a student smiles while receiving critical feedback.
```

The smile may represent confidence, embarrassment, politeness, anxiety, or an attempt to manage social tension.

Research value:

- Education technology
- Coaching tools
- Conversation analysis
- Human-computer interaction research
